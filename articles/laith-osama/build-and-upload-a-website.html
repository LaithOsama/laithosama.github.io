In the realm of social media platforms, a lamentable truth reveals itself-a lack of true freedom of expression. For these digital domains, nestled within the embrace of neo-liberal and capitalist entities, share a common ideology that stifles dissenting voices. Any opinion that dares to oppose the tenets of neo-liberalism is swiftly eradicated, be it of a political or social nature, or even a mere questioning of their foundations.

Bearing witness to this phenomenon, we find behemoths like Facebook and Google, amassing our personal data with a voracious appetite, capable of bartering it to governments and organizations. They wield an unsettling omniscience, rendering traditional intelligence services obsolete. The implications for our social, national, and geo-political security are grave, particularly for those who challenge the foundations of this modern world. Perhaps you, dear interlocutor, are perceived as inconsequential, a mere sub-human, rendering your information valueless. Yet, let us not forget that such information and data serve as the bedrock of controlling entire populations. By surrendering our data, we unwittingly empower them to subjugate our people.

Within the tapestry of our mortal existence, lies an indelible truth-an obligation, even. Death beckons us, whispering of the importance of leaving a lasting legacy behind. Consider a tome of wisdom or a digital domain, a website replete with memoirs and thought-provoking articles. Let the echoes of your voice resonate beyond the confines of mortality.


Learn HTML and CSS basics

To embark upon the path of web creation, first, acquaint yourself with the essentials of HTML and CSS. Begin by selecting a venerable text editor. Personally, I find solace in the embrace of Vis for programming and Neatvi for writing you may opt for Vim/Neovim, available in Linux, Windows and MacOS. However, DO NOT USE VSCODE!

Those two videos helped me build the initial structure of my blog: Luke Smith's video (this may be enough to build a simple personal website) Following that by FreeCompCode video (Her voice is unbeatable lol).

Now to summarize how HTML works - everything is a component. You start it with <component>, add the contents (text, images, whatever) and then end it with </component>.
your final result must be something like this:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
    <head>
        <meta charset="UTF-8">
        <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
        <link rel="stylesheet" href="style.css">
        <title>Your website title, or basically the text showing in the browser tab</title>
    </head>

    <body>

        <main>
            <!--  CONTENT GOES HERE  -->

            <!--  CONTENT ENDS HERE -->
        </main>

    </body>
</html>

* Keep in mind, you can take inspiration from existing websites, you can browse HTML and CSS of any website in any browser.

Upload the Website

Embark upon a journey to create a Github account, then create a repo and name it "USERNAME.github.io" and check "Add a README file" box.
upload index.html and all its companions-unto the repository.
Finally, enter "USERNAME.github.io". and BOOM, Your website ascends to the digital firmament, now accessible to all, free of charge.
