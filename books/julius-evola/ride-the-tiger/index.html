Chapter 1: Introduction

In this chapter, Evola introduces the concept of the "tiger," which represents a force that is both destructive and creative. He argues that modern society has lost touch with this force and that individuals must learn to "ride the tiger" in order to achieve a higher state of being.

Chapter 2: The "Animal" Aspect of the Human Being

Evola explores the animalistic nature of humans and argues that this aspect of our being has been suppressed by modern society. He suggests that individuals must embrace their animal nature in order to achieve a higher state of consciousness.

Chapter 3: The Dissolution of the Individual in the Modern World

In this chapter, Evola discusses the ways in which modern society has eroded the individual's sense of self. He argues that individuals must resist this dissolution and strive to maintain their individuality in order to achieve a higher state of being.

Chapter 4: The "Dionysian" Path

Evola explores the concept of the "Dionysian" path, which involves embracing chaos and destruction in order to achieve a higher state of consciousness. He argues that this path is necessary for individuals who wish to "ride the tiger."

Chapter 5: The "Apollonian" Path

In contrast to the Dionysian path, the Apollonian path involves embracing order and structure in order to achieve a higher state of consciousness. Evola argues that both paths are necessary for individuals who wish to achieve a higher state of being.

Chapter 6: Active Nihilism

Evola discusses the concept of "active nihilism," which involves rejecting the values of modern society and creating one's own values. He argues that this is necessary for individuals who wish to achieve a higher state of consciousness.

Chapter 7: The Transcendent Dimension and the "Mysteries"

In this chapter, Evola explores the concept of the transcendent dimension and the role of "mysteries" in achieving a higher state of consciousness. He argues that individuals must transcend the material world in order to achieve a higher state of being.

Chapter 8: The "Royal Art"

Evola discusses the concept of the "royal art," which involves the use of symbols and rituals to achieve a higher state of consciousness. He argues that this art is necessary for individuals who wish to "ride the tiger."

Chapter 10: The "Way of Knowledge"

Chapter 11: The Conquest of the State

In this chapter, Evola discusses the role of the state in modern society and argues that individuals must work to conquer the state in order to achieve a higher state of being.

Chapter 12: Conclusion

Evola concludes by summarizing his arguments and emphasizing the importance of "riding the tiger" in order to achieve a higher state of consciousness.
