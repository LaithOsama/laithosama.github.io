The Yoga of Power: Tantra, Shakti, and the Secret Way

Julius Evola

Philosophy, Spirituality.

Chapter 1: The Doctrine of Awakening

In the opening chapter of "The Yoga of Power" by Julius Evola, the author introduces the concept of the doctrine of awakening. Evola explores the spiritual path of awakening and the attainment of higher states of consciousness through the practice of yoga.

Evola's philosophy emphasizes the importance of transcending the limitations of the ordinary mind and ego, and developing a direct connection with the transcendent realities through disciplined practice and self-transformation.

Chapter 2: The Forces and Their Symbols

This chapter delves into the forces and their symbols as understood in the context of yoga. Evola explores the symbolism and significance of various forces and energies encountered on the path of spiritual awakening.

Evola's philosophy encourages individuals to understand and work with these forces, cultivating awareness, and harnessing their transformative power to achieve higher levels of consciousness.

Chapter 3: The Way of Power

In this chapter, Evola discusses the way of power in yoga. He explores the dynamic and active approach to spiritual practice, focusing on the cultivation of willpower, self-mastery, and the awakening of inner power.

Evola's philosophy encourages individuals to embrace the path of power as a means of transcending limitations, overcoming obstacles, and realizing their full spiritual potential.

Chapter 4: The Way of Action

This chapter focuses on the way of action in yoga. Evola explores the role of action, service, and selfless dedication in spiritual practice, emphasizing the importance of karma yoga and the integration of action and contemplation.

Evola's philosophy highlights the transformative power of selfless action, recognizing it as a means to purify the ego, cultivate detachment, and align one's actions with higher principles.

Chapter 5: The Yoga of the Subtle Body

In the final chapter, Evola delves into the yoga of the subtle body. He explores the subtle energy centers, channels, and practices that facilitate the awakening of spiritual energies and the expansion of consciousness.

Evola's philosophy encourages individuals to explore and cultivate the subtle aspects of their being, working with energy practices and visualization techniques to access higher states of consciousness and inner transformation.

Book Recommendations

  1. “The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali” translated by Swami Satchidananda: This classical text provides a systematic guide to the practice of yoga, including ethical principles, meditation techniques, and the attainment of spiritual liberation.
  2. “Kundalini: The Arousal of the Inner Energy” by Ajit Mookerjee: This book explores the concept of Kundalini energy and its awakening, offering insights into the practices and experiences associated with this transformative force.
  3. “The Secret of the Yoga Sutra: Samadhi Pada” by Pandit Rajmani Tigunait: This commentary on the Yoga Sutras provides in-depth explanations and practical guidance for the practice of meditation and the attainment of samadhi.
  4. “The Serpent Power: The Secrets of Tantric and Shaktic Yoga” by Arthur Avalon: This book explores the practices and philosophy of Kundalini Yoga, delving into the awakening of the dormant spiritual energy and its ascent through the subtle body.
  5. “Autobiography of a Yogi” by Paramahansa Yogananda: Yogananda's memoir offers a personal account of his spiritual journey and encounters with various yogis and masters, providing insights into the transformative power of yoga and meditation.
