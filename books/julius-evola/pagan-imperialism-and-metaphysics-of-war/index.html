Pagan Imperialism and the Metaphysics of War

Julius Evola

Philosophy, Politics, Spirituality.

Chapter 1: The Revolt Against the Modern World

In the opening chapter of “Pagan Imperialism” by Julius Evola, the author explores the concept of a revolt against the modern world. Evola critiques the decline of traditional values and the erosion of spiritual and cultural foundations, emphasizing the need for a resurgence of ancient wisdom.

The revolt against the modern world represents a rejection of the materialistic and egalitarian values of modernity. Traditional thought recognizes the importance of reconnecting with the timeless wisdom and spiritual traditions of the past to restore harmony and transcendence.

Chapter 2: Tradition and Empire

This chapter delves into the relationship between tradition and empire. Evola discusses the role of traditional values and spirituality in the formation and maintenance of great empires throughout history, highlighting the significance of imperial ideals.

Tradition and empire are intertwined, as empires often emerge from civilizations rooted in deep spiritual and cultural traditions. Traditional thought emphasizes the need for a transcendent vision that guides the empire and provides a higher purpose beyond mere political and economic ambitions.

Chapter 3: The Imperial Idea

In this chapter, Evola explores the concept of the imperial idea and its manifestation in different cultures. He examines the qualities and virtues associated with the imperial ideal, such as heroism, honor, and a sense of higher destiny.

The imperial idea embodies the noble qualities and virtues that contribute to the flourishing of civilizations. Traditional thought recognizes the importance of individuals and societies aligning themselves with these higher ideals to establish and maintain an empire.

Chapter 4: The Sacred Nature of the Empire

This chapter delves into the sacred nature of the empire. Evola discusses the spiritual and metaphysical dimensions of the empire, emphasizing its connection to higher realms and its role as a vessel for divine principles.

The empire is seen as a sacred institution that reflects the higher order and cosmic harmony. Traditional thought emphasizes the importance of upholding spiritual values, conducting sacred rituals, and recognizing the divine presence within the empire.

Chapter 5: The Imperium of the West

In the final chapter, Evola focuses on the Imperium of the West. He explores the historical and spiritual foundations of the Western civilization, discussing its unique qualities, challenges, and potential for a resurgence.

The Imperium of the West represents the manifestation of Western civilization's imperial potential. Traditional thought emphasizes the need for the West to reconnect with its spiritual heritage, uphold its traditional values, and play a significant role in the global order.
