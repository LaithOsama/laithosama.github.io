Revolt Against the Modern World

Julius Evola

Philosophy, Politics, Spirituality.

Chapter 1: The Modern World in Crisis

In the opening chapter of “Revolt Against the Modern World” by Julius Evola, the author explores the crisis of the modern world. Evola critiques the decline of traditional values, the erosion of spiritual and metaphysical principles, and the emergence of a mechanistic and materialistic worldview.

The crisis of the modern world is seen as a deviation from the timeless principles and spiritual foundations that once guided human civilization. Traditional thought emphasizes the need for individuals to transcend the limitations of the modern worldview and reconnect with the transcendent and higher truths.

Chapter 2: The Traditional Worldview

This chapter focuses on the traditional worldview as a response to the crisis of the modern world. Evola explores the metaphysical, spiritual, and hierarchical principles that form the basis of traditional societies, emphasizing the importance of regaining a traditional perspective.

From a traditional point of view, the traditional worldview represents a holistic understanding of reality that acknowledges the transcendent dimensions of existence. Traditional thought emphasizes the hierarchical structure of the cosmos, the importance of spiritual development, and the preservation of traditional values and social order.

Chapter 3: The Revolt of the Individual

In this chapter, Evola discusses the concept of the revolt of the individual against the modern world. He explores the different forms this revolt can take, from nihilism and destructive rebellion to the possibility of a higher spiritual revolt.

The revolt of the individual can be seen as a reaction to the spiritual emptiness and materialistic focus of the modern world. Traditional thought emphasizes the importance of a spiritual revolt that seeks higher values, transcendence, and the restoration of a higher order beyond the confines of the individual ego.

Chapter 4: The Path of Initiation

This chapter delves into the path of initiation as a means of transcending the limitations of the modern world. Evola discusses the importance of initiation rituals, esoteric knowledge, and spiritual disciplines in the journey toward higher states of consciousness.

Initiation represents a transformative process through which individuals can transcend the limitations of the profane world and access higher levels of awareness and understanding. Traditional thought emphasizes the cultivation of spiritual disciplines, the mastery of esoteric knowledge, and the pursuit of inner transformation.

Chapter 5: The Return of Tradition

In the final chapter, Evola discusses the possibility of a revival and restoration of traditional values and principles. He explores the role of individuals in working toward the resurgence of a traditional worldview and the establishment of a new spiritual and social order.

The return of tradition involves a collective effort to restore the timeless principles and spiritual foundations that have been eroded by the modern world. Traditional thought emphasizes the importance of individuals aligning themselves with higher principles and participating in the revitalization of traditional values.

  1. “Ride the Tiger” by Julius Evola: In this book, Evola explores the concept of the "ride the tiger" as a metaphor for navigating the challenges of the modern world with a traditional mindset and spiritual resilience.
  2. “Metaphysics of War” by Julius Evola: This book delves into the metaphysical and spiritual dimensions of warfare, exploring its role in shaping societies and individuals and its connection to the traditional warrior ideal.

  4. “The Crisis of the Modern World” by René Guénon: Guénon's book offers a critique of modernity from a traditional perspective, analyzing the spiritual and metaphysical dimensions of the crisis facing contemporary civilization.
  5. “The World as Will and Representation” by Arthur Schopenhauer: Schopenhauer presents his philosophy of the will as the underlying force behind all phenomena, exploring the implications of this perspective for understanding the nature of reality and human existence.
