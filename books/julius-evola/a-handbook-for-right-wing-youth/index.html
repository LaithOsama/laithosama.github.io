A Handbook for Right-Wing Youth

Julius Evola

Politics, Philosophy.

Chapter 1: The Crisis of Modernity

In the opening chapter of “A Handbook for Right Wing Youth” by Julius Evola, the author addresses the crisis of modernity and its impact on youth. Evola critiques the decline of traditional values, the loss of spiritual meaning, and the erosion of cultural identity.

The crisis of modernity represents a departure from the timeless principles and virtues that once guided society. Traditional thought emphasizes the need for youth to resist the conformist pressures of modern culture and to reconnect with their ancestral heritage and transcendent values.

Chapter 2: The Right Wing Youth Movement

This chapter explores the emergence of the right wing youth movement as a response to the crisis of modernity. Evola discusses the importance of youth engaging in political activism, cultural preservation, and the restoration of traditional values.

The right wing youth movement represents a reawakening of the spirit of youth to defend and uphold the timeless traditions and values of their culture. Traditional thought emphasizes the cultivation of character, discipline, and a sense of purpose in the youth's engagement with the political and cultural sphere.

Chapter 3: Spiritual and Physical Training

In this chapter, Evola emphasizes the significance of spiritual and physical training for right wing youth. He discusses the importance of developing inner strength, self-discipline, and a strong connection to one's ancestral roots.

Spiritual and physical training are seen as essential components in the formation of a well-rounded individual. Traditional thought emphasizes the cultivation of virtues, the pursuit of knowledge, and the development of physical and mental resilience.

Chapter 4: Cultural Activism

This chapter delves into the role of cultural activism in the right wing youth movement. Evola discusses the importance of preserving and promoting traditional cultural expressions, art, and literature as a means of countering the forces of cultural decay.

Cultural activism serves as a means to preserve and revitalize the collective memory and identity of a people. Traditional thought emphasizes the cultivation of beauty, creativity, and the transmission of cultural heritage to future generations.

Chapter 5: The Metaphysics of Right Wing Youth

In the final chapter, Evola explores the metaphysical foundations of the right wing youth movement. He discusses the transcendent principles and spiritual dimensions that guide the youth in their pursuit of a higher ideal.

The metaphysics of right wing youth emphasizes the connection between the individual, their culture, and the transcendent realm. Traditional thought emphasizes the cultivation of virtue, the pursuit of excellence, and the alignment with cosmic and divine principles.

Book Recommendations

  1. “The Revolt of the Masses” by José Ortega y Gasset: This book explores the challenges posed by mass society and the erosion of individuality, offering insights into the importance of maintaining a sense of personal identity and distinction.
  2. “The Conservative Mind” by Russell Kirk: Kirk traces the history of conservative thought, highlighting the key principles and figures that have shaped conservative ideologies and movements.
  3. “The Doctrine of Fascism” by Benito Mussolini: This book presents Mussolini's vision of fascism, addressing its key principles, political philosophy, and its relevance to the right wing movement.
  4. “Men Among the Ruins” by Julius Evola: Evola's book explores the decline of modern civilization and offers a critique of the prevailing cultural and political trends, providing insights into the traditionalist perspective.
  5. “The Metaphysics of War” by Julius Evola: In this book, Evola delves into the metaphysical and spiritual aspects of warfare, examining its role in shaping societies and individuals, and its connection to the traditional warrior ideal.
