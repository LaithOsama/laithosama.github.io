Chapter 1: The Dark Age

In this chapter, Guénon discusses the concept of the Dark Age, or Kali Yuga, as a period of decline and degeneration in human history. He argues that the modern world is experiencing this dark age, characterized by materialism, individualism, and the loss of traditional values. Guénon emphasizes the need for a return to spiritual principles and the importance of understanding the cyclical nature of history in order to overcome the crisis of the modern world.

Chapter 2: The End of a Cycle - "The Twilight of the West"

Guénon explores the idea that the modern Western civilization is approaching the end of a historical cycle, marked by a decline in spiritual values and an increase in materialism. He compares the current state of the West to the decline of ancient civilizations and suggests that the West must rediscover its spiritual roots in order to avoid a similar fate.

Chapter 3: The Confusion of the Social Orders

In this chapter, Guénon examines the disintegration of traditional social structures and the rise of individualism in modern society. He argues that this confusion of social orders has led to a loss of hierarchy and order, resulting in chaos and instability. Guénon calls for a return to traditional social structures based on spiritual principles in order to restore balance and harmony in society.

Chapter 4: The Destruction of the Crafts

Guénon discusses the decline of craftsmanship and the rise of industrialization in the modern world. He argues that the loss of traditional crafts has led to a devaluation of work and a focus on materialism and profit. Guénon emphasizes the importance of preserving traditional craftsmanship and its spiritual significance in order to counteract the negative effects of industrialization.

Chapter 5: The Degeneration of the Arts and Letters

In this chapter, Guénon examines the decline of art and literature in the modern world, arguing that they have become superficial and devoid of spiritual meaning. He suggests that the arts and letters must return to their traditional roots and rediscover their spiritual purpose in order to regain their true value and significance.

Chapter 6: The Disruption of the Traditional Framework of Existence

Guénon discusses the breakdown of traditional ways of life and the rise of modernity, which has led to a loss of connection with the spiritual world. He argues that this disruption of the traditional framework of existence has resulted in a crisis of meaning and purpose in the modern world. Guénon calls for a return to traditional ways of life based on spiritual principles in order to restore balance and harmony in society.

Chapter 7: The Fissures in the Great Wall

In this chapter, Guénon explores the weakening of the barriers between the spiritual and material worlds, resulting in the infiltration of materialism into the spiritual realm. He argues that this has led to a decline in spiritual values and an increase in materialism, contributing to the crisis of the modern world. Guénon emphasizes the need to strengthen the barriers between the spiritual and material worlds in order to restore balance and harmony.

Chapter 8: Some Consequences of the Present State of Affairs

Guénon discusses the various consequences of the crisis of the modern world, including the rise of atheism, materialism, and individualism. He argues that these consequences have led to a decline in spiritual values and a loss of meaning and purpose in life. Guénon calls for a return to spiritual principles and traditional ways of life in order to overcome the crisis of the modern world.
