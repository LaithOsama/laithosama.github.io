The Reign of Quantity and the Signs of the Times

René Guénon

Philosophy, Religion, Politics.

Chapter 1: The Darkness of the Times

In the opening chapter of “The Reign of Quantity and the Signs of the Times” by René Guénon, the author introduces the concept of the "darkness of the times". Guénon critiques the decline of traditional values, the rise of materialism, and the prevalence of quantitative thinking in modern society.

Guénon's philosophy highlights the need to recognize and transcend the limitations of a purely quantitative and materialistic worldview, and to seek a deeper understanding of the spiritual dimensions of reality.

Chapter 2: The Dissolution of Substance

This chapter delves into the "dissolution of substance". Guénon explores the erosion of traditional metaphysical principles and the reduction of reality to its superficial and material aspects.

Guénon's philosophy emphasizes the importance of recognizing the illusory nature of the material world and seeking a higher understanding of the spiritual essence that underlies all manifestations.

Chapter 3: The Signs of the Times

In this chapter, Guénon examines the "signs of the times". He explores the various manifestations and symbols that reflect the decline of traditional values and the dominance of quantitative and materialistic thinking.

Guénon's philosophy encourages individuals to interpret the "signs of the times" as indicators of a deeper spiritual crisis and to awaken to the need for a return to traditional wisdom and metaphysical principles.

Chapter 4: The Reign of Quantity

This chapter focuses on the concept of the "reign of quantity". Guénon explores the prevalence of quantitative thinking in modern science, philosophy, and social structures, and its implications for the understanding of reality.

Guénon's philosophy critiques the limitations of a purely quantitative approach, emphasizing the need to integrate qualitative and metaphysical dimensions in order to grasp the true nature of existence.

Chapter 5: The Modern Deviation

In the final chapter, Guénon discusses the "modern deviation" from traditional principles. He examines the consequences of the loss of metaphysical foundations and the need to restore a proper understanding of reality.

Guénon's philosophy calls for a return to traditional wisdom, the recognition of the transcendent dimensions of existence, and the restoration of metaphysical principles in order to address the spiritual crisis of the modern world.

Book Recommendations

  1. “The Crisis of the Modern World” by René Guénon: Guénon's book offers a critique of modernity from a traditional perspective, analyzing the spiritual and metaphysical dimensions of the crisis facing contemporary civilization.
  2. “The Symbolism of the Cross” by René Guénon: In this work, Guénon explores the symbolic and metaphysical significance of the cross across different religious and spiritual traditions, providing insights into its deeper meanings.
  3. “The Metaphysical Principles of the Infinitesimal Calculus” by René Guénon: Guénon examines the metaphysical and symbolic aspects of mathematics, particularly focusing on the concept of the infinitesimal calculus.
  4. “The Essential René Guénon: Metaphysics, Tradition, and the Crisis of Modernity” edited by John Herlihy: This collection of essays provides an overview of Guénon's key ideas and contributions to traditionalist thought.
  5. “The Science of the Sacraments” by René Guénon: Guénon explores the symbolism and spiritual significance of sacraments in different religious traditions, offering insights into their role in spiritual transformation.
