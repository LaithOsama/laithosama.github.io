Alexander Dugin

Born in Moscow in 1962, Alexander Dugin is a Russian philosopher and political analyst known for his controversial views. His philosophy combines elements of traditionalism, geopolitics, and esotericism.

Dugin's Philosophy

Dugin's philosophy is centered around the concept of "Eurasianism," which advocates for a multipolar world order based on distinct civilizational blocs. He sees Western liberalism as a threat to cultural diversity and advocates for a return to traditional values and societal structures.

Reading Guide

  • “The Fourth Political Theory” - In this book, Dugin presents his alternative to liberal democracy, communism, and fascism, advocating for a multipolar world order based on cultural and civilizational diversity.
  • “The Foundations of Geopolitics” - This work provides an overview of Dugin's geopolitical theories and their implications for global politics.
