Alexander Dugin

Chapter 1: The Crisis of Modernity

In the first chapter of Political Platonism, Alexander Dugin argues that the modern world is in a state of crisis. He argues that this crisis is due to the decline of traditional values and the rise of individualism.

Dugin argues that traditional values, such as hierarchy, order, and community, are essential for the well-being of society. He argues that individualism, on the other hand, leads to chaos, disorder, and conflict.

Dugin argues that the only way to overcome the crisis of modernity is to return to traditional values. He argues that this can be done by establishing a new political order based on the principles of Platonic political philosophy.

Chapter 2: The Philosophy of Plato

In the second chapter of Political Platonism, Dugin examines the philosophy of Plato. He argues that Plato's philosophy provides a blueprint for the ideal political order.

Dugin argues that Plato's ideal political order is a hierarchical society in which the rulers are philosophers. He argues that the philosophers are the only ones who are capable of understanding the true nature of reality and of ruling in the interests of the common good.

Dugin argues that Plato's philosophy is still relevant today. He argues that the principles of Platonic political philosophy can be used to create a new political order that will overcome the crisis of modernity.

In the third chapter of Political Platonism, Dugin discusses the future of political Platonism. He argues that political Platonism is a living tradition that has been adapted to the needs of different times and cultures.
