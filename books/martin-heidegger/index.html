Martin Heidegger

Born in Germany in 1889, Martin Heidegger was one of the most influential philosophers of the 20th century. His work is characterized by a deep exploration of the nature of Being and the human experience.

Heidegger's Philosophy

Heidegger's philosophy is centered around the concept of "Dasein," or "being-there," which refers to the experience of being that is peculiar to human beings. Heidegger believed that modern technology and the emphasis on rationality in Western philosophy have led to a forgetfulness of Being, resulting in a state of existential alienation.

Reading Guide

  • “Being and Time” - This is Heidegger's most influential work, in which he explores the concept of "Dasein" and the nature of human existence.
  • “The Question Concerning Technology” - In this work, Heidegger examines the impact of modern technology on our understanding and experience of Being.
