Mission Statement

An independent, non-profit digital library run by one person, I hold it in my hands for the benefit of my nation, hoping that it will benefit the community and make it an ongoing charity for anyone who participates in it.

Perhaps you have noticed the problems with Arabic digital libraries, which usually include books of little value (mostly novels and human development classifications) filled with advertisements of all kinds and javascript. Along with poor programming, they are difficult to use and may take minutes to download a single book. With all of this and more in mind, I have decided to create a digital library that:

  1. Provides Arabic translation whenever available, accompanied by their English counterparts, shielding readers from the perils of substandard translations.
  2. Provides books that work to increase cognitive awareness, broaden your horizons, and move away from unhelpful novels and human development works.
  3. Focuses more on books that support tradition and discourage progress, with attention paid to religious books as well.
  4. Attempts to find the best software solutions to make the site as fast and clean as possible.
  5. Does not include any ads and javascript at all.
  6. Ease and speed of use, as one of the priorities of the project is to provide the possibility of acquiring knowledge even for those who do not have experience and knowledge in technology and the Internet..
